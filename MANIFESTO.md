AI evaluation has followed a clear arc. Each generation of AI systems was tested against a wider slice of the world. First floats. Then vectors. Then unbounded text. Then audio, images, and every other kind of digital content. Today, AI systems are expected to interact with the full variety of the digital world, and with the physical world through sensors and actuators. To properly evaluate them, we need environments that span that entire surface. At the same time, we need those environments to be reproducible, isolated, and governed.

Early evaluation frameworks ran the model and the task in the same thread. As agents grew more complex, frameworks separated them into distinct processes. As the environments around them grew richer, each process moved into its own container. A container for the agent, one for the inference service, one for the MCP server, and more for the supporting infrastructure. Databases, web services, application engines. Without noticing, existing evaluation frameworks like Exgentic, Harbor, and Inspect reinvented docker-compose. The natural next step is to just use docker-compose.

That is eval-containers. It is not a framework. It is a curated set of the most widely used benchmarks and agents in the industry, shipped as standalone containers, with docker-compose files to orchestrate their evaluation end to end. You run `docker compose up` and the original code of the agent and the benchmark are running. You own the containers and the compose files completely. Deploy them on your favorite cloud or local infra. If the eval-containers repo is deleted tomorrow, you lose nothing. Without a single line of our code, you keep running your evaluations forever.

We don't maintain a framework. Our mission is quality. Agent testing is tricky. Even top players routinely expose evaluation code to the agent in ways that bias results. Quality is the hard problem, not orchestration. Our sole focus is the fidelity of our container fleet. The repo is the quality process itself, governed by clear rules, readable by both humans and agents, designed for incremental improvement.

Use eval-containers. We have the benchmarks you need. The agents you need. All verified with a quality assurance process we stand behind. The only way to keep raising that bar is through use and careful inspection. Testing AI systems is not a nice to have. It is how we make sure the future works. We better compose our future carefully, wrap it in safer containers, and ship it with care.
